Cyber Hygiene Guide
Your complete guide to spotting phishing emails, protecting yourself online, and building good security habits. Knowledge is your best defence.
🎣 How to Spot a Phishing Email
THE 8 WARNING SIGNS YOU MUST KNOW
📧
Suspicious sender address
The display name says "PayPal" but the actual email is [email protected] or paypa1.com. Always check the full address, not just the name.
⏰
Urgency and panic tactics
"Your account will be deleted in 24 hours!" Attackers create panic so you act without thinking. Legitimate companies don't do this.
🔗
Suspicious or shortened URLs
Hover over any link before clicking. If the URL doesn't match the company (e.g. bit.ly/bank-login), don't click it. Go directly to the website instead.
👤
Generic greetings
"Dear Customer" or "Dear User" instead of your real name. Real companies that have your account know your name and use it.
📎
Unexpected attachments
An invoice you didn't request, a "document" from someone you don't know. Attachments can contain malware that installs silently when opened.
💳
Requests for sensitive info
No legitimate bank, government body, or company will ever ask for your password, PIN, or full card details via email. Ever.
✍️
Poor grammar and spelling
Professional companies proofread their emails. Typos, awkward phrasing, and inconsistent formatting are signs of a rushed scam operation.
🎁
Too-good-to-be-true offers
"You've won £500!" or "Claim your free iPhone." If you didn't enter a competition, you didn't win anything. These harvest your personal details.
⚖️ Do's and Don'ts
WHAT TO DO AND AVOID WHEN YOU RECEIVE A SUSPICIOUS EMAIL
✓ DO THIS
🔍
Verify independently. If your bank emails you, close the email and go directly to your bank's website by typing the address yourself.
📞
Call the company directly using the number on their official website — not the number in the suspicious email.
🚩
Report phishing emails to your email provider. In Gmail, click the three dots → "Report phishing." This protects other users too.
🗑️
Delete and move on. Once you've identified a phishing email, delete it immediately. Don't forward it to friends "to warn them" — that spreads it.
✗ NEVER DO THIS
🔑
Never enter your password on a page you reached by clicking a link in an email. Go to the site directly instead.
💰
Never send gift cards or money to anyone you met online or via email. No legitimate employer, government body, or prize gives gift cards.
📎
Never open unexpected attachments — especially .exe, .zip, .docm files. Even if the sender looks legitimate, their account could be compromised.
🤫
Never keep it to yourself. If you almost fell for a phishing email, tell your family, friends, or colleagues. You might save someone else.
🚨 What To Do If You Got Phished
ACT FAST — EVERY MINUTE COUNTS
⚡
Don't panic — but act immediately. If you clicked a link, entered credentials, or downloaded an attachment, follow these steps in order. The faster you act, the less damage occurs.
1
Disconnect from the internet immediately
If you opened a malicious attachment, disconnect your WiFi or ethernet right away. This stops malware from sending your data to attackers or spreading across your network.
2
Change your passwords immediately
Change the password for the account that was targeted first. Then change passwords for any other accounts that use the same password. Do this from a different, trusted device.
3
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Even if an attacker has your password, 2FA means they still can't log in without your phone. Enable it on every important account — email, banking, social media.
4
Contact your bank if financial info was shared
Call your bank's fraud line immediately. They can freeze your card, monitor for suspicious transactions, and reverse unauthorized charges if you act fast enough.
5
Run a full antivirus scan
Reconnect to the internet and run a full system scan with your antivirus. If you don't have one, Windows Defender (built into Windows) is reliable. Remove anything flagged.
6
Report to authorities and your email provider
In Nigeria, report to the EFCC cybercrime unit or send to [email protected]. Also report to your email provider so they can warn others receiving the same phishing campaign.
🔐 Password & Account Security
BUILDING STRONG DEFENCES BEFORE AN ATTACK HAPPENS
🔑
Strong Passwords
Use at least 12 characters mixing letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use your name, birthday, or "password123". A passphrase like Coffee!Sunrise#2026 is strong and memorable.
📱
Two-Factor Authentication
Enable 2FA on every account that supports it. Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) rather than SMS where possible. SMS can be intercepted via SIM swapping.
🗂️
Password Manager
Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) or 1Password. It generates unique passwords for every site and remembers them for you. This means one compromised password doesn't affect all your accounts.
🔄
Regular Security Checkups
Visit haveibeenpwned.com to check if your email appears in any data breaches. Review which apps have access to your Google/Microsoft account and remove ones you no longer use.
